First Aid Tips for Emergencies

❤️ Heart Attack +

Recognize the signs: Chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, nausea, lightheadedness, pain in arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach.

Immediate actions:

  • Call emergency services immediately (977-1-5970099)
  • Have the person sit down, rest, and try to stay calm
  • Loosen any tight clothing
  • If the person is not allergic to aspirin, give them 325mg to chew slowly
  • If the person stops breathing, begin CPR if trained
🧠 Stroke +

Remember FAST:

  • Face drooping - Does one side of the face droop or feel numb?
  • Arm weakness - Is one arm weak or numb?
  • Speech difficulty - Is speech slurred or hard to understand?
  • Time to call emergency services - If someone shows any of these symptoms, call immediately.

Additional signs: Sudden numbness, confusion, trouble seeing, difficulty walking, severe headache.

🔥 Burns +

For minor burns (first-degree):

  • Cool the burn under cool (not cold) running water for 10-15 minutes
  • Apply aloe vera gel or moisturizer
  • Cover with sterile gauze bandage
  • Take over-the-counter pain reliever if needed

For major burns (second/third-degree):

  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Do NOT remove burnt clothing
  • Do NOT immerse large severe burns in cold water
  • Cover with clean, dry cloth or bandage
  • Watch for signs of shock
🦴 Fractures/Broken Bones +

Signs of a fracture: Intense pain, swelling, bruising, deformity, inability to move the injured area.

First aid steps:

  • Do not move the person unless necessary to avoid further injury
  • Call emergency services or go to the nearest emergency department
  • If trained, apply a splint to immobilize the area
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling (not directly on skin)
  • Elevate the injured area if possible
🤐 Choking +

Signs of choking: Inability to speak, difficulty breathing, weak cough, clutching throat, bluish skin color.

First aid for conscious adult/child:

  • Give 5 back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand
  • If not effective, give 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
  • Continue alternating 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts until object is dislodged or person becomes unconscious

If person becomes unconscious:

  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Begin CPR with chest compressions
  • Look for the object in the mouth before each breath
🤒 Allergic Reactions +

Signs of severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis): Difficulty breathing, swelling of face/lips/tongue/throat, hives, rapid pulse, dizziness, loss of consciousness.

Immediate actions:

  • Call emergency services immediately
  • If available, use an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) as directed
  • Have the person lie flat with legs elevated
  • Loosen tight clothing and cover with a blanket
  • If vomiting occurs, turn person on their side to prevent choking
  • If breathing stops, begin CPR
